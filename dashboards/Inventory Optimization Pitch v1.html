Excess stock

Working capital

Our approach

Material by material. Grounded in actual consumption.

We don't model. We don't forecast. We read what your fleet has already done over the past year of MB51 history, calculate the consumption rate of every qualifying material, and use that rate — and only that rate — to recommend Min/Max.

Step 01
Pull movement history

Twelve-plus months of MB51 transactions, joined to IW39 work orders so we count only fleet-related consumption (movement types 261, 201). Returns and corrections net out.

MB51 · IW39 · Fleet Master
Step 02
Measure the gradient

Two consumption windows per material: a five-month baseline (P1) and a rolling three-month current (P2). The delta is your real demand trajectory, not a memory of last year.

P1 base · P2 rolling · per-material rate
Step 03
Recommend & flag

Recommended Min = P2 rate × min months. Recommended Max = P2 rate × max months. Every line gets a traffic-light call with a clear next action — no inference, no "smart" overrides.

Min/Max recommendation · 6-light triage

When the slope changes, the settings have to change with it.

The slope is changing

9.7 / mo  3.2× the baseline — sustained, not a one-off spike
Stock-out exposure
Min/Max anchored on P1 underestimates demand by ~14 units over the same window — a guaranteed shortfall under the current rate.

High Consumption Events

Not all consumption is equal. We separate signal from event.

One rebuild can spike a part 50× normal demand.

An undercarriage rebuild, a sprocket changeout, a hose replacement at major service — these are real work orders, but they don't repeat next month. A naive Min/Max tool averages the spike in. Yours doesn't need to.

Our pipeline flags any work order that consumed ≥ 50% of the period total or ≥ 3× the average WO quantity. The flagged events are listed alongside the chart, with WO number, description, equipment, and date — so the planner sees exactly what spiked the rate and decides whether to apply the raw or the adjusted rate.

Why this matters The raw P2 rate above includes a 30-unit undercarriage rebuild. The adjusted rate (HCE excluded) is 4.1/mo, not 6.8. That's the difference between recommending a Max of 41 units or 25.

Recommendation system

Six signals. Every part lands on one of them.

GREEN
Settings already optimal

Recommended Min and Max match current. No action — confirm in next review cycle.

BLUE
Excess inventory — safe to lower

Both rec values fall below current. Lower Min/Max to release working capital. Lowest-risk action category.

ORANGE
Under-stocked — raise to avoid stockout

Rec exceeds current, or current is mixed. Increase Min/Max — these are your stockout-risk parts. Highest priority.

RED
Wrong MRP type for the demand

Currently PD (manual reorder) but consumes regularly. Convert to V1 with proper Min/Max — eliminates judgment calls on routine parts.

WR?
Possible seasonal / winter-road

PD with high stock and low recent draw. Likely seasonal. Planner reviews — could be appropriate as-is. Surfaced, not auto-changed.

GREY
Insufficient data or out of scope

No P2 activity, missing from Inventory Master, or unusual MRP type. Surfaced for manual review — never silently skipped.

Logic is deterministic and priority-ordered — every line gets exactly one light, no overlaps, no inference.
