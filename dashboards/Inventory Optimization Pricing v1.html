The market

Three competing markets — none of them is the right shape for what we sell.

Three distinct categories chase the same problem with different economics. Birchwood's positioning gap sits between them — too service-heavy for SaaS comparison, too lean and SAP-specific for MBB transformation programs.

How the market prices

Four pricing structures in play. We use two — and a third optionally.

01
Subscription / SaaS

Flat annual fee, priced by plants / systems / inventory value. Predictable revenue, software-led, requires integration. Software maintenance norm: 15–20% of license per year (Forrester).

IBM Maximo IO · Verusen · ToolsGroup · Slimstock · Syncron · SAP IBP
02
Fixed-fee project

Scoped one-time engagement, milestone payments. Most consulting work. Sustain phase often priced separately. Birchwood's per-fleet sprint fits here.

McKinsey · BCG · Big-4 diagnostics · Stratflow
03
T&M day rates

Bill by consultant-day. Engineering / EPCM norm. Big-4 G-Cloud rates: SM £1,706–£2,120, Partner £2,500–£3,800. Mining-specialist senior $1,500–$2,500/day (inferred).

SRK · Hatch · Worley · all engineering specialists
04
Gain-share / contingent

Base fee + % of validated savings. Procurement norm: 20–40% of validated savings, often tapered (Spend Matters). Specific splits never publicly disclosed. Optional layer on Birchwood recurring.

Partners in Performance (heritage) · Stratflow (partial fee-at-risk) · McKinsey RTS results-oriented
What Birchwood uses A fixed-fee per-fleet sprint as the baseline shape · a recurring service retainer for ongoing optimization · an optional gain-share variant on the recurring layer for sophisticated clients with verifiable working-capital outcomes.

  • No live-data integrationManual SAP exports, not API. We don't see consumption in real time.

Optional upside · summary

A gain-share variant aligns our economics with the outcome.

Gain-share variant
on the recurring layer

For sophisticated clients with verifiable working-capital outcomes. Defensible structure:

Year 1
Base recurring at 60–70% of standard price + 15–25% of verified working-capital release.
Year 2
Tapers to a flat retainer once the working-capital release has been realized and the new MRP regime is steady-state.
Precedent PARTNERS IN PERFORMANCE  built mining heritage on contingent fees · STRATFLOW  puts portion of fees at risk · MCKINSEY RTS  offers results-oriented turnarounds. Cross-industry procurement gain-share norm: 20–40% of validated savings, often tapered.

Bottom line

Position
Defensible gap in a thinly-priced market — between SaaS subscriptions ($37K/yr Maximo Essentials) and Big-4 transformation programs ($1–2M+ diagnostics).
Biggest risk
Pricing the floor too low. Below $60K per fleet positions us as a software replacement (we aren't), invites comparison to Maximo Essentials, and undervalues the senior-advisor cost build.
Biggest opportunity
The gain-share variant. Clear mining precedent (PiP, Stratflow). Aligns our economics with the working-capital outcomes the methodology directly produces.
Next step
Confirm which engagement shape leads with the first opportunity — fixed-fee sprint as the default anchor, gain-share variant held in reserve for the right client conversation.
