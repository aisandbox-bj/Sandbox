    Team NumaCore — Operating Manual
    How to Work With Your Team
    Three ways to get things done. Pick the right channel for the right task — and the team handles the rest.
    📥 Route A — Through Dean Recommended for most tasks
    1
    Drop the task into the Ideas column
    On the Operations Board, click + Add Card in the Ideas column. Give it a clear title — think of it as the subject line of an email to Dean. Use the Notes field to add context, links, constraints, or anything Dean needs to understand the job.
    2
    Open a new Claude session and address Dean directly
    Start a fresh Claude conversation and begin with: "Dean — [card title]. Here's the context: [paste your notes]." Dean reads your briefing, asks any clarifying questions, and decides what happens next — who gets the work, in what order, and what outputs to expect.
    New Claude session Paste card notes as context
    3
    Dean triages and routes to the right agent
    Dean will parse your request, break it into subtasks if needed, and either handle it himself or hand off to Atlas, Vance, Orion, Rex, Sage, or whoever owns that domain. He may come back to you with a clarifying question — answer it, and he continues. Move the card to In Progress once Dean is on it.
    4
    Update the board at session end
    When Dean delivers the output, move the card to Done This Week. If something is waiting on you (a decision, a file, an approval), move it to Waiting on Jay so it doesn't get lost. The board is your shared memory — keeping it current takes 30 seconds and saves hours.
    Move to Done when complete Waiting on Jay if blocked on you
    🎯 Route B — Direct to an Agent When you know exactly who you need
    1
    Open a new Claude session and name the agent
    Start with the agent's name — "Atlas — I need you to review this code..." or "Vance — I need a competitor analysis for..." Each agent has a SKILL.md in the Roles & Responsibilities folder that defines their identity and how they work. You can paste that in as a system prompt for best results.
    2
    Give them full context up front
    Agents work best with a complete briefing in one go — the goal, the constraints, any relevant background, and what a good output looks like. You can skip the card for direct work, but adding it to the board anyway keeps your overall picture accurate.
    Use SKILL.md as system prompt Attach memory.md for ongoing context
    🔁 Route C — Scheduled Agent Work For recurring tasks that run themselves
    1
    Set up a scheduled task for recurring work
    Some agents have standing jobs — Dean's risk register review, Orion's process audit, Vance's competitive monitoring. These can be set up as scheduled Claude tasks that run on a cadence (daily, weekly) and drop their output wherever you need it. Ask Dean to help design any new recurring workflow.
    2
    Review the output and update the board
    When a scheduled agent delivers its report, you review, decide if action is needed, and either drop a new card on the board or park it in the Parking Lot for later. Orion owns the maintenance of these cycles — if a schedule is broken or needs adjusting, route it through him.
    Orion owns schedules Park non-urgent outputs
    💡
    The golden rule: every task Jay wants actioned needs two things — a card on the board (so nothing gets lost) and a Claude session where you hand off with context. The card is the record. The session is the conversation. Together, they keep the whole team in sync and nothing falls through the cracks.
